\title{WISE: Wrench- and Information-Self-Sustaining Equilibria\\
for Heterogeneous Multi-Robot Coalitions}

\author{\IEEEauthorblockN{Jorge Luis Mayorga Taborda}
\IEEEauthorblockA{\textit{Universidad Militar Nueva Granada}\\
Bogot\'a, Colombia\\
jl.mayorga236@gmail.com}}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
A Nash-stable coalition is not operationally meaningful if it cannot generate
the required wrench, or if its own spatial deployment destroys the communication
network needed to compute and maintain that equilibrium. We introduce the
\emph{Wrench-and-Information Self-Sustaining Equilibrium} (WISE), a refinement of
the generalized variational equilibrium that couples three requirements:
strategic stability, physical wrench feasibility, and sustainability of the
\emph{induced} communication graph. The formulation rests on a directional
wrench-capacity tensor and an endogenous Laplacian that is affine in the team's
decisions, so the information constraint $\lamtwo(\Lap(x))\ge\sigmastar$ is a
convex super-level set. We give an exact tensor-aggregative potential, prove
existence and team-optimality of WISE, and expose two certification prices---one
for force/torque, one for connectivity. A distributed primal--dual algorithm
converges to a WISE and, in simulation of heterogeneous robot teams (convergence,
phase diagram, constraint ablation, emergent roles), shows that wrench feasibility
and information self-sustainability are jointly, not separately, necessary, and
that relay roles emerge from radius heterogeneity alone. \emph{A WISE equilibrium
can both execute itself and compute itself.}
\end{abstract}
