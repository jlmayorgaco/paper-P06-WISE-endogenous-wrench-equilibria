\title{WISE: Self-Sustaining Population Equilibria\\
over Decision-Induced Robot Networks}

\author{\IEEEauthorblockN{Jorge Luis Mayorga Taborda}
\IEEEauthorblockA{\textit{Universidad Internacional de Valencia (VIU)}\\
Valencia, Spain\\
jl.mayorga236@gmail.com}}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Distributed population-game dynamics need a connected communication graph to
estimate the aggregates and payoffs that define their equilibria. We study the
self-referential case in which the equilibrium's own spatial composition
determines whether that graph exists. For a heterogeneous multi-robot team we
show that the productively optimal aggregate is unique but its \emph{composition}
is degenerate---a positive-dimensional face of equal-payoff profiles---so
classical theory fixes how much wrench each load receives, not which robot types
provide it. On that face we place an endogenous, position-induced Laplacian and
define the \emph{Wrench- and Information-Self-Sustaining Equilibrium} (WISE): a
productively optimal composition that also keeps algebraic connectivity above the
Nash-seeking threshold $\sigmastar=\vt^2/(c\,\mF)$. We prove (i) composition
degeneracy, (ii) that a self-sustaining equilibrium exists iff
$\Lammax:=\max_{x\in\Emanifold}\lamtwo(\Lap(x))\ge\sigmastar$, (iii) an exact
local stability threshold $c\,\mF\,\lamtwo>\vt^2$ derived from the coupled
estimation dynamics, and (iv) that an infinitesimal information reward selects
the most-connected composition without moving the productive optimum. Closed-loop
simulation with rigid-load transport and second-order unicycles confirms all
four, and shows relay roles emerging from radius heterogeneity by comparative
advantage. \emph{A population equilibrium can produce a configuration that
destroys the graph its own algorithm requires; composition heterogeneity is
exactly what lets it select the equilibria that survive.}
\end{abstract}
