\section{Distributed Algorithm}
\label{sec:algorithm}

We seek a WISE by a projected primal--dual flow that couples a population revision
protocol to the connectivity price of Corollary~\ref{cor:prices}:
\begin{subequations}\label{eq:pd}
\begin{align}
\dot x_\tau &= \mathcal R_\tau\!\Big(x,\;\nabla_{x_\tau}V(x)
+\pi\,\nabla_{x_\tau}\lamtwo(\Lap(x))\Big),\label{eq:pd-x}\\
\dot\pi &= \big[\sigmastar-\lamtwo(\Lap(x))\big]_+^{\pi},\label{eq:pd-pi}
\end{align}
\end{subequations}
where $\mathcal R_\tau$ is a mass-preserving revision protocol and the wrench price
$\mu_k=\nabla F_k(s_k)$ is internal to $\nabla V$. Each type needs only its own
capability rows $\Wmap_{\tau,\cdot}$, the aggregate deficit $d-s(x)$, and the local
Fiedler sensitivity $v^{\top}L_e v$, all obtained by consensus over the current
graph. Economically: a missing wrench direction raises $\mu$; missing connectivity
raises the relay price $\pi$; mass migrates toward the highest type-action value
\eqref{eq:prices}.

\paragraph*{Choice of revision protocol}
We use a projected-ascent (Euclidean) protocol, which is Nash-stationary: its rest
points are exactly the constrained maximisers of $V$~\cite{sandholm2010population}.
Smith and BNN protocols share this property. We deliberately avoid the replicator
(multiplicative-weights) map: it is \emph{not} Nash-stationary on the boundary---it
can rest at support-restricted profiles that leave a profitable but extinct action
unused, and it never repopulates a mass it has driven to
zero~\cite{sandholm2010population}. On a face $\Emanifold$ whose self-sustaining
subset may require re-activating a relay type, replicator can therefore stall short
of $\Ess$; projected ascent cannot.

\begin{proposition}[Convergence to a WISE]\label{prop:conv}
Assume $\Phi_0$ strongly concave, Slater feasibility of $\Ess$, and a
Nash-stationary revision $\mathcal R$. Then the saddle points of the Lagrangian of
$\max_{x\in\Ess}V$ coincide with the WISE profiles, and \eqref{eq:pd} is a
projected monotone flow whose trajectories converge to one, provided the consensus
gain keeps the estimator above the stability threshold of
Theorem~\ref{thm:stability} throughout.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}[Sketch]
Strong concavity of $V$ and convexity of $\Ess$ make the Lagrangian
concave--convex, so its saddle points are the KKT (hence WISE) points by
Corollary~\ref{cor:prices}. The map in \eqref{eq:pd} is the projected gradient of
this saddle function, which is monotone; passivity arguments for primal--dual
Nash-seeking give convergence~\cite{gadjov2019passivity,facchinei2003fvi}. The
proviso is exactly \eqref{eq:threshold}: if the trajectory drops below $\sigmastar$
the estimator error grows (Theorem~\ref{thm:stability}) and the aggregates fed to
$\mathcal R$ are no longer trustworthy---the algorithm can only be guaranteed to
converge where the equilibrium is self-computing.
\end{proof}

The proviso is the paper's point in algorithmic form: convergence of the
distributed method is contingent on the equilibrium it is converging to being
information self-sustaining.
