\section{Distributed Algorithm}
\label{sec:algorithm}

The headline solver is a projected primal--dual flow that realizes the
certification prices of Corollary~\ref{cor:prices}:
\begin{subequations}\label{eq:pd}
\begin{align}
\dot x_i &=\Pi_{T_{\Delta_i}(x_i)}\!\Big[\nabla_{x_i}\Pot(x)+A_i^{\top}\mu
+\pi\,\nabla_{x_i}\lamtwo(\Lap(x))\Big],\label{eq:pd-x}\\
\dot\mu  &=\Pi_{\R_+}^{\mu}\!\big[d-s(x)\big],\label{eq:pd-mu}\\
\dot\pi  &=\Pi_{\R_+}^{\pi}\!\big[\sigmastar-\lamtwo(\Lap(x))\big],\label{eq:pd-pi}
\end{align}
\end{subequations}
where $A_i$ collects the tensor slices $\Wten_{ia,k\ell}$ and
$\Pi_{T_{\Delta_i}(x_i)}$ is the projection onto the simplex tangent cone. Each
robot needs only its own slices, the aggregate deficits $d-s(x)$, and the local
Fiedler sensitivity $v^{\top}L_e v$, all obtainable by consensus over the current
graph. The reading is economic: a missing wrench direction raises its price
$\mu$; missing connectivity raises the relay price $\pi$; slack on either
constraint lets its price fall; robots migrate toward the actions of highest
marginal contribution~\eqref{eq:marginal}.

\begin{proposition}[Convergence to a WISE]\label{prop:conv}
Under the hypotheses of Theorem~\ref{thm:existence} with $\Pot$ strictly concave
in the aggregates and Slater's condition on $\Xwise$, the saddle points of the
Lagrangian of $\max_{x\in\Xwise}\Pot$ coincide with the WISE profiles, and the
flow~\eqref{eq:pd} is a projected pseudo-gradient of a monotone saddle operator;
its trajectories converge to such a saddle point.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}[Sketch]
Concavity of $\Pot$ and convexity of the constraints make the Lagrangian
concave--convex, so its saddle points are exactly the KKT (hence WISE) points by
Corollary~\ref{cor:prices}. The map in \eqref{eq:pd} is the projected gradient of
this saddle function, which is monotone; passivity/monotone-operator arguments for
primal--dual Nash-seeking then give convergence~\cite{gadjov2019passivity,facchinei2003fvi}.
\end{proof}

Replacing the projected-gradient primal map \eqref{eq:pd-x} by a replicator
(multiplicative-weights) map leaves the rest-point set unchanged; we use the
projected form throughout.
