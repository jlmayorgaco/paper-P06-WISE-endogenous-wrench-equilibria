\section{Simulation Study}
\label{sec:exp}

We simulate teams of $N=12$ heterogeneous robots in a two-region world: a load in
the right cluster demands a planar wrench $\wdem=(0,\text{lift},\tau_d)$, while
short-range links connect robots only within a cluster, so the inter-cluster gap
is bridged only when a \emph{long-range} robot commits to the $\mathrm{relay}$
action---activating its full range. Robots differ in force capacity $F_i$ and
communication range $r_i$; a fraction $\nu$ are long-range. Each instance yields a
directional wrench tensor \eqref{eq:tensor} and an endogenous Laplacian
\eqref{eq:laplacian}, and is solved by the distributed primal--dual flow
\eqref{eq:pd}. The primary metric is the \emph{certified service rate}: a load
counts as served only if it is both wrench-feasible and information
self-sustaining ($\max_\ell[d_\ell-s_\ell]_+\le\epsilon_w$ \emph{and}
$\lamtwo-\sigmastar\ge-\epsilon_I$). All instances are seeded; the campaign
reproduces exactly.

\paragraph*{Convergence (Fig.~\ref{fig:conv})}
On a representative instance the distributed dynamics drive $\lamtwo(\Lap(x))$
onto the threshold $\sigmastar$ and the wrench residual to zero
simultaneously---the equilibrium becomes both wrench-feasible and self-computing.
The information price $\pi$ settles at its certification value, and the relay mass
concentrates entirely on long-range robots: the roles are priced into existence,
not assigned.

\begin{figure}[t]\centering
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{fig2_convergence.pdf}
\caption{Distributed primal--dual convergence. (a) $\lamtwo\!\to\!\sigmastar$ and
the wrench residual $\to0$ together. (b) The information price $\pi$ converges and
relay mass accrues to long-range robots only (short-range stays at zero).}
\label{fig:conv}
\end{figure}

\paragraph*{Phase diagram (Fig.~\ref{fig:phase})}
Sweeping the long-range fraction $\nu$ and torque demand $\tau_d$ (12 seeds per
cell), the certified service rate collapses in two regimes predicted by the
theory: at $\nu\!\to\!0$ no robot can bridge the gap, so wrench-feasible
coalitions are \emph{information self-defeating}; at high $\tau_d$ the fleet is
wrench-limited. Certification survives only in the interior WISE band, and its
left edge is exactly the connectivity-limited boundary.

\paragraph*{Constraint ablation (Table~\ref{tab:ablation})}
We compare five solvers on paired seeds at a WISE-feasible operating point.
Enforcing either constraint alone fails: \texttt{scalar\_capacity} and
\texttt{wrench\_only} build valid coalitions that fragment the network (negative
margin), while \texttt{connectivity\_only} keeps the network but cannot lift the
load (large residual). Only the WISE primal--dual solver certifies both, matching
the centralized oracle---evidence that the distributed equilibrium attains the
team optimum of Theorem~\ref{thm:existence}.

\paragraph*{Emergent roles (Fig.~\ref{fig:roles}b)}
Pooling the WISE solutions across seeds, relay commitment is drawn
\emph{exclusively} from long-range robots: no short-range robot ever relays,
while the long-range robots with the best connectivity-value-to-cost ratio take
up the relay role. Comparative advantage in radius---not a hand-coded
assignment---produces the division of labour, exactly as the price system
\eqref{eq:prices} predicts.
