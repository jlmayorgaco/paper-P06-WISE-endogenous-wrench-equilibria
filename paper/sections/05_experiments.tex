\section{Simulation Study}
\label{sec:exp}

We validate the four results on heterogeneous teams ($N=12$ unless noted) in a
two-region world: a rigid load in the right cluster demands a planar wrench, and
short links connect robots only within a cluster, so the gap is bridged only when
a long-range type relays. The strategic layer runs the primal--dual flow
\eqref{eq:pd}; the physical layer integrates a rigid load and second-order
unicycles, with each lifter's contact force confined to a heading-aligned
elliptical set (nonholonomic: free along the heading, limited laterally), so wrench
realizability genuinely depends on pose. The primary metric is the \emph{certified
service rate}: a load is served only if it is wrench-feasible \emph{and}
$\lamtwo-\sigmastar\ge0$. Every instance is seeded and reproduces exactly.

\paragraph*{E1 --- Convergence (Fig.~\ref{fig:conv})}
On a representative instance the distributed flow drives $\lamtwo(\Lap(x))$ onto
$\sigmastar$ and the wrench residual to zero together: the equilibrium becomes
wrench-feasible and self-computing at once. The information price $\pi$ settles at
its certification value and the relay mass accrues \emph{only} to long-range types.

\begin{figure}[t]\centering
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{fig2_convergence.pdf}
\caption{Distributed convergence. (a) $\lamtwo\!\to\!\sigmastar$ and wrench residual
$\to0$ together. (b) Price $\pi$ converges; relay mass accrues to long-range types
only (short-range stays at zero).}
\label{fig:conv}
\end{figure}

\paragraph*{E2 --- Self-defeat threshold (Fig.~\ref{fig:thresh}, Thm.~\ref{thm:stability})}
On one instance we compare two compositions from the same face $\Emanifold$. When a
long-range robot relays, the induced $\lamtwo(t)$ rises above $\sigmastar$ and the
coupled estimation error $\|e(t)\|$ decays; when it is withdrawn, $\lamtwo(t)$ stays
below and $\|e(t)\|$ grows---the equilibrium defeats its own estimator. The sign
change occurs exactly at $c\,\mF\,\lamtwo=\vt^2$, the derived threshold, not a
fitted curve.

\begin{figure}[t]\centering

(B) vs.\ self-defeating (A) composition. (b) Estimation error decays iff
$c\,\mF\lamtwo>\vt^2$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:stability}); it diverges below the threshold.}
\label{fig:thresh}
\end{figure}

\paragraph*{E3 --- Existence phase diagram (Fig.~\ref{fig:phase}, Thm.~\ref{thm:existence})}
Sweeping the long-range fraction $\nu$ and torque demand $\tau_d$ (12 seeds/cell),
certification collapses exactly where predicted: at $\nu\!\to\!0$ no composition on
$\Emanifold$ reaches $\Lammax\ge\sigmastar$ (existence fails), and at high $\tau_d$
the fleet is wrench-limited. The certified band is the region where
$\Lammax\ge\sigmastar$.

$\nu\!\to\!0$) and wrench feasibility (fails at high $\tau_d$).}

\paragraph*{E4 --- Constraint ablation (Table~\ref{tab:ablation}, Fig.~\ref{fig:roles}a)}
On paired seeds, enforcing either requirement alone fails: \texttt{scalar\_capacity}
and \texttt{wrench\_only} build valid coalitions that fragment the network,
\texttt{connectivity\_only} keeps the network but cannot lift, and only WISE
certifies both---matching the centralized oracle, evidence that the distributed
selection attains $\max_{x\in\Ess}V$.

\paragraph*{E5 --- Emergent roles (Fig.~\ref{fig:roles}b, Prop.~\ref{prop:selection})}
Pooling WISE solutions across seeds, relay commitment is drawn \emph{exclusively}
from long-range types: no short-range robot ever relays. The price system
\eqref{eq:prices} selects, within the degenerate face $\Emanifold$, the composition
that maximises connectivity---comparative advantage in radius produces the division
of labour, exactly the infinitesimal-reward selection of
Proposition~\ref{prop:selection}.
