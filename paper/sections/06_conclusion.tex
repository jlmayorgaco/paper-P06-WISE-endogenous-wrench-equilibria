\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We studied population equilibria whose own spatial composition determines whether
the communication graph their algorithm requires exists. Three facts make the
phenomenon tractable and sharp: the productive optimum fixes the aggregate but
leaves a positive-dimensional composition face (Theorem~\ref{thm:degeneracy}); on
that face a self-sustaining equilibrium exists iff its best induced connectivity
clears the Nash-seeking threshold (Theorem~\ref{thm:existence}); and that same
threshold is the exact stability boundary of the coupled estimator
(Theorem~\ref{thm:stability}), so an infinitesimal information reward selects the
surviving composition (Proposition~\ref{prop:selection}). Closed-loop simulation
with rigid-load transport and second-order unicycles confirms all four and shows
relay roles emerging by comparative advantage. We extend the distributed
population-game and generalized-Nash machinery of the Sandholm and
Quijano--Barreiro-G\'omez--Mart\'inez-Piazuelo
lines~\cite{sandholm2010population,quijano2017role,barreiro2016constrained,%
martinezpiazuelo2022} to the case where the decision generates the graph that
machinery presupposes.

\paragraph*{Limitations}
The analysis is a \emph{fluid relaxation}; integer assignments are recovered by
rounding under the margin condition of Remark~\ref{rem:integer}. The induced
Laplacian is affine in the decision (candidate-site relaxation of the
position-dependent graph); the fully continuous-position geometry $L(q)$ is
nonconvex and is exercised only in simulation. Nonholonomic wrench realizability is
modelled by the heading-aligned force set, not proved; and validation is in
simulation.

\paragraph*{Future work}
A realizability theorem for the nonholonomic contact, the nonconvex
continuous-position graph, and hardware validation are the natural next steps
toward a full closed-loop guarantee.
