\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

% Budget: ~0.75 page (with references).

We introduced WISE, an equilibrium refinement in which a coalition is
operationally meaningful only when it is strategically stable, wrench-feasible,
and able to sustain the induced communication graph that computes and maintains
it. A directional wrench tensor and an affine, endogenous Laplacian make the
feasible set convex; an exact tensor-aggregative potential yields existence,
team-optimality, and two certification prices---one for force/torque, one for
connectivity. Experiments show wrench feasibility and information
self-sustainability are jointly necessary, and that relay/torque roles emerge
from heterogeneity alone.

\paragraph*{Limitations}
The role-induced graph is affine rather than fully pairwise-quadratic;
$\lamtwo$ is assumed simple at the operating point; the study is in simulation.

\paragraph*{Future work}
A Nash--Lagrange treatment with wrench-market prices and a closed-form vGNE with
explicit sensor-to-motor control are deferred to companion papers.
